2.8 Descriptive and inferential statistics

Descriptive statistics: The branch of statistics that includes methods for organizing and summarizing data. Statistical tools and ideas help us examine data to describe their main features. This examination is called exploratory data analysis.

Here are two basic strategies that help us organize our exploration of a set of data:

  • Begin by examining each variable by itself. Then move on to study the relationships among the variables.
  • Begin with a graph or graphs. Then add numerical summaries of specific aspects of the data.

Inferential statistics: The branch of statistics that involves generalizing from a sample to the population from which the sample was selected and assessing the reliability of such generalizations.
